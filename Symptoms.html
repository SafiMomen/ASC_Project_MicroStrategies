Possible Symptoms & Events Of Someone Contemplating Suicide:

  • Wanting to die
  • Lots of shame and/or guilt
  • Feeling like their life has no meaning
  • Misuse of alcohol and drugs
  • Social isolation
  • Death of a loved one
  • Serious financial problems
  • Stress resulting from prejudice and discrimination (family rejection, bullying, violence)
  • Lack of access to behavioral health care
  • Severe sadness or bipolarness (extreme mood swings)
  • Feeling a deep sense of hopelessness about the future
  • Empty, hopeless, trapped, or having no reason to live
  • Extremely sad, more anxious, agitated, or full of rage
  • Unbearable emotional or physical pain
  • Making a plan or researching ways to die
  • Withdrawing from friends, saying goodbye, giving away important items, or making a will
  • Taking dangerous risks such as driving extremely fast
  • Eating or sleeping more or less
  • Using drugs or alcohol more often
  • Changes in personality or appearance
  • Childhood abuse and trauma
